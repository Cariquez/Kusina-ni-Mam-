“MAMÉ’S SPECIAL MANGO FLOAT”

Mamé’s special Mango float is a luscious Filipino dessert made with layers of ripe mangoes, graham crackers, and creamy whipped cream. It's a refreshing and indulgent treat that's perfect for satisfying your sweet cravings on a hot day.

Filipino mango float is a delightful no-bake dessert that features layers of cream, mango, and graham crackers. While it's incredibly easy to prepare, the anticipation of enjoying it is almost too much to bear! Refrigerating the dessert overnight is essential to allow the cream to set and the crackers to soften.

This dessert is at its best when made with ripe, fresh mangoes. As someone who isn't particularly skilled at baking, I found assembling this mango float to be a breeze. With just 4 ingredients, it's light, creamy, and perfectly sweet – making it the perfect treat for those scorching summer days.

INGREDIENTS

  1. Ripe mangoes, sliced thinly
  2. Graham crackers (or graham cracker crumbs)
  3. Whipped cream
  4. Sweetened condensed milk

These simple ingredients come together to create a delicious and refreshing Filipino dessert that's loved for its creamy texture and tropical flavor.



"STEP 1 : Preparing Your Ingredients"


  • Prepare your ingredients by first slicing the ripe mangoes thinly. Though consistency may differ for most, mamé always finds it nice to take note that the more mangoes you add to your mango float the better it will taste!

  • Once you finish slicing up your mangoes go ahead and start combining your whipped cream and sweetened condensed milk.

    • Note:

    The more you add the sweeter they will be. Mamé always likes to add at least half or the whole can depending on how sweet the ripe mangoes are.

    Give it a taste and add your sweetened condensed milk to your preferred level off sweetness.

"STEP 2 : Arranging"
  • In a shallow dish or container, spread arrange your graham crackers as your base, making sure to fill the edges as well.
  • Now on top of your grahams go ahead and drizzle a generous amount of sweetened whipped cream before topping

    Note:

    Go crazy! Add and arrange as many or less mangoes to your choosing.

    Once that's finished proceed to top the mangoes with your sweetened whipped cream and repeat the process until your container is filled to the top.

    (optional: add some more mangoes as your toppings and sprinkle with crushed grahams)

"STEP 3 : The Long Wait"
  • Mango Float's are best served COLD. Once your float is ready, be sure to cover it and let it chill in the freezer for a good long hour or two. Crucial, but once that good long wait is over, I promise you, it's all worth it.

    • Once chilled, take it out, grab yourself a slice, and try your hardest not to hog it all to yourself. Enjoy!
